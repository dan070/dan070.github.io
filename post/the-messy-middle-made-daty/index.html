The messy middle and how to make it data-sciency

Last updated on 2021-08-17 5 min read

Google’s take on messiness

About 10 years after the “Zeroth moment of truth” (dubbed ZMOT… giggle), Google does a retake of the same ideas. Their research report: “Decoding Decisions, messy middle” came out July 2020.

The feeling they want you to have after reading about the “Messy middle” is of ‘Charting undiscovered territory’ (a headline in the text). However, they are not going as much upwind, as they would want you to feel. To prove that, let’s go back 10 years.

The ZMOT that came before

Rereading now, ten years after publication, ZMOT comes across as part marketing science and part marketing campaign tutorial. It’s also part ‘Digitalisation 101’ course, with lines such as:

…now there’s no friction. You can pull out your mobile phone and look it up.

…people take the time to leave messages online about how much they love Scotch Tape. That’s because the effort is down to zero.

Teach the old marketing dogs about digital

The ZMOT also included a whole section of counter-arguments to objections from marketing managers: “It doesn’t apply to my industry.” or “We already have a website. We’re doing fine.”

One headline reads: ‘The rise of video’ and contains this (prudent) remark :

Today’s young adults have had mobile-phone cameras in their pockets since they were old enough to have pockets.

Other admonitions of hurry-up-and-get-digitalized read:

You’d never set up a corporate 1-800 number with nobody to answer the phone. (Would you?) … The Internet is that 1-800 number, and it’s been set up for you even though you didn’t ask for it.

Maybe they were aiming to open they eyes of old-school marketers. It’s a little paternal. But probably an effective communication mode.

A playbook to usher digital marketing in

The ZMOT makes for a great manual on how to sell SEO and ads to marketing managers! Like this: (1) Point to a changing world, (2) give a new model for marketing, (3) prove that it’s actually true, (4) counter any arguments, and then (5) give concrete advice on what to do. Finish with (6) a safari of the future.

The messy middle update, versus the ZMOT

So, what has changed since ZMOT? And what is new in the “messy middle” (shortened to MM here)

The basic story of internet shoppers is unchanged

The basic story remains, in both the MM and ZMOT. Going from stimulus - into the internet - out via an order. The MM has a cleaner conceptualisation. And methods of actionability. The ZMOT was heavier on anecdotes and interviews with satisfied Google-customers.

The empiric premise in both texts are the same: “We asked a bunch of people how they shop online. This is what we found”. Also, a good part of the MM text goes through how to explore markets with Google Trends and the resulting conclusions with some 20 figures. In this sense, it’s partly “open” research (as in ‘open software’).

Cognitive bias research has been made a cornerstone

The MM language feels more academic, with words like ‘consideration sets’, ‘intersection’ and ‘hypothesis’. These words don’t even occur in the ZMOT text.

The new jargon of behavioural science has heavily influenced the MM, like ‘reward system’, ‘cognitive inputs’ etc. Specifically, the MM is more clear on how to influence the buyers using cognitive shortcuts while the ZMOT is more like a chatty mentor. The “cognitive biases” could be found (scattered and buried) in the ZMOT text, but now they form the bulk of a clearly though-out experiment.

What others say

There are a few different opinions, here for example where critics point out:

  • the ethical sides of using cognitive biases (e.g. Darth Vader also uses these skills)

  • the simple fact that Google (in both texts) reaches conclusions favorable to their own search/ads products.

Others here find that they’ve personally been ahead of Google all this time.

  • Some have been teaching these very ideas for the last 20 years.

  • Their big epiphany (whenever that happened) is that consumers/people are now empowered and not passive.

But most [here] (https://www.thedrum.com/opinion/2020/10/28/digital-marketer-s-guide-google-s-messy-middle) seem to be in agreement that

  • the Google study is a ‘fresh perspective’ but might not ‘replace a good strategy’

The discovery of Kahneman

The firm that Google collaborated with, seems inspired by Kahneman et al (using his language of System 1 and System 2 thinking on their web pages). Tversky and Kahneman’s book would, it seems, have been transformed into a specialist marketing company.

The next step

As I’m partly working in the field, I’m setting out to make a data science model of the MM. The model would be just a framework, to be filled with data from a specific marketing department.

The model data could be from various systems, but the model coefficients would reflect the specific customer behavior in the specified market under consideration.

On top, I’ll try to fit in the effects of interventions on the different stages. This is the usual focus of analytics, but I’m looking for a context that can also determine the effect size.

The method I plan to use will be predominately Bayesian on aggregated data.

The next post in this trail will be inserted here. So, stay tuned =)
